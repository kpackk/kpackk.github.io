How to Choose Curtains in Dubai: Complete Guide 2026

Dubai is a city with a unique climate -- the sun shines over 300 days a year, and summer temperatures soar above 45 degrees Celsius. In these conditions, curtains are far more than a decorative touch -- they become a necessity. They shield your home from the blazing sun, reduce the load on your air conditioning, and create a comfortable living atmosphere. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to choose the right curtains for your Dubai home, taking into account the climate, local architecture, and your budget.

Curtain Types for Dubai

The UAE market offers several main curtain types, each designed for specific needs. Sheer curtains are lightweight, semi-transparent fabrics that gently diffuse daylight while providing privacy. They're a staple in almost every apartment and work especially well in living rooms with panoramic windows where you want to preserve a sense of openness and natural light.

Blackout curtains are dense, multi-layered fabrics that block up to 99% of sunlight. They're essential for bedrooms, particularly those with east- or south-facing windows. In Dubai, blackout curtains also significantly reduce air conditioning costs by preventing heat from entering the room.

Day-night curtains feature alternating transparent and opaque strips, allowing you to smoothly adjust the light level. They're a versatile solution for rooms that need different levels of brightness throughout the day. Roman shades fold into neat horizontal pleats and fit perfectly into minimalist interiors. They take up minimal space and are ideal for kitchens and bathrooms.

Choosing the Right Fabric

In Dubai's climate, fabric choice is critically important. Natural materials -- linen, cotton, silk -- create a premium feel and breathe well. However, they're more susceptible to fading under intense UV exposure. For sun-facing windows, blended fabrics with synthetic fibres are a better choice: they maintain their appearance much longer.

Synthetic and technical fabrics -- polyester, acrylic, specialised sun-protection materials -- offer high UV resistance and easy maintenance. They don't fade, wash easily, and dry quickly. Blackout curtains use triple-layered fabrics with a special coating that reflects sunlight and keeps the interior cool.

What to Look for When Ordering

Accurate measurement is the foundation of a great result. Even a small error can lead to curtains that sag or fail to fully cover the window. A professional measurer accounts for not just the window opening, but also ceiling height, sill depth, furniture placement, and AC unit locations. That's why we recommend having a specialist take your measurements rather than doing it yourself.

Pay attention to the mounting method: grommets, loops, header tape, or hooks -- each creates a different visual effect. Ask the company about production timelines, warranty terms, and what's included in the price. A reputable manufacturer includes not just the curtain tailoring, but also the curtain rod, delivery, and installation in the total cost.

How Much Do Curtains Cost in Dubai?

Curtain prices in Dubai depend on several factors: fabric type, window size, mounting method, and whether you want motorization. Approximate 2026 prices: sheer curtains in organza or voile -- from 150 AED per square metre, blackout curtains -- from 200 AED/m², day-night curtains -- from 180 AED/m², Roman shades -- from 250 AED/m². Motorization adds from 350 AED per window.

The final price is also affected by the fabric manufacturer, curtain rod complexity, and number of windows in the order. When outfitting multiple rooms at once, most companies offer a discount.
