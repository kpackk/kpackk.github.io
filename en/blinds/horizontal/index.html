Horizontal Blinds: Classic and Practical

Horizontal blinds, also known as Venetian blinds, are one of the oldest and most time-tested window treatment systems. They work on horizontal slats connected by cords or ladders. The slat angle is adjusted using a tilt wand, while raising and lowering is done with a cord or chain.

Solara Design offers two main materials for horizontal blinds. Aluminum slats (0.21 mm thick) are coated with heat-resistant enamel that won't fade or peel even under constant sun exposure. The palette includes over 80 colors: from classic white and silver to wood and stone texture imitations.

Wooden horizontal blinds are crafted from solid bamboo, basswood, or Canadian cedar. Each slat is coated with multiple layers of lacquer that protects the wood from drying out in Dubai's air-conditioned environments. Wooden blinds provide natural thermal insulation and create a distinctive atmosphere of warmth in any interior.

Horizontal blinds are especially popular in bathrooms and kitchens. Aluminum models don't absorb moisture at all and are easily cleaned with a sponge and soapy water. For spaces with heightened hygiene requirements, we offer slats with antibacterial coating. Professional installation by Solara Design ensures secure mounting and proper mechanism operation throughout the product's lifespan.

Horizontal Blinds FAQ

Helping you make the right choice

Aluminum blinds are more practical: they're moisture-resistant, won't warp from temperature changes, and cost less. Ideal for kitchens, bathrooms, and offices. Wooden blinds (bamboo, basswood, cedar) look more luxurious and create a warmer atmosphere. They're best installed in living rooms and bedrooms.
Yes, aluminum horizontal blinds are perfectly suited for bathrooms. They are fully moisture-resistant and corrosion-proof. For especially humid spaces, we recommend models with a special anti-corrosion coating. Wooden blinds are not recommended for bathrooms due to sensitivity to humidity.
For aluminum blinds, slat widths of 16 mm (mini, for small windows), 25 mm (standard, most popular), and 50 mm (maxi, for large windows) are available. Wooden slats come in 25, 35, and 50 mm widths. Our specialist will help you select the optimal width for your window size.
